Local Expertise

Why Shasta Area Homes Need
Specialized Painters

The Shasta area — home to the Shasta State Historic Park and one of California's earliest Gold Rush communities — has a housing stock that carries significant age and history. Properties along Highway 299 West, Placer Road, and the Igo-Ono area include structures that date back decades, with layers of old paint, weathered wood, and in many cases the presence of lead-based paint on homes built before 1978.

Lead paint is a serious concern that amateur painters frequently overlook. Improper disturbance of lead paint can create health hazards. We perform thorough surface assessment on all older properties and follow EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, and Painting) safe work practices. You deserve to know what's on your walls before any sanding, scraping, or preparation begins.

The 2018 Carr Fire left a significant mark on this area. Many homes near Whiskeytown and the Shasta-Redding corridor underwent rebuilding or major renovation — and new construction in a fire-prone area benefits from exterior coatings with fire-retardant properties. We can discuss these options with you during your estimate visit.

Shasta's slightly cooler temperatures compared to the valley floor — combined with oak woodland and chaparral surroundings — create a moderate but demanding climate for exterior paint. We select the right systems for these specific conditions and back every project with a 5-year warranty.
