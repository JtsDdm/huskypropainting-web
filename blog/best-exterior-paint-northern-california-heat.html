Paint Selection

Best Exterior Paints for Northern California's Heat (2025)

By Husky Pro Painting · Redding, CA · Updated July 2025 · 7 min read

Redding, California is one of the hottest cities in the continental United States. Summers regularly push past 110°F, the UV index is punishing from May through September, and the temperature can swing 40–50 degrees between a summer afternoon and the following morning. That combination is brutal on exterior paint — and it means the brand and formula you choose matters far more here than in any other climate in the country.

We've been painting homes in Redding and throughout Shasta County since 2020. In that time, we've seen exactly which paints hold up and which ones chalk, crack, and fade within two or three years. This guide gives you our honest, field-tested recommendations.

Bottom line up front: For Redding's climate, only use 100% acrylic exterior paint from a premium line. Budget and mid-grade paints simply cannot handle the UV load and thermal stress. The extra $15–$25 per gallon you spend on premium paint translates to years of added protection.

Why Paint Fails Faster in Northern California

Before we name brands, it's worth understanding why the wrong paint fails so quickly in this region. There are three compounding forces working against your exterior paint in Redding:

1. Extreme UV Radiation

Redding sits in a valley with relatively little cloud cover for most of the year. UV radiation breaks down the binder and pigment in exterior paint, causing it to chalk (a powdery surface residue), fade, and lose adhesion. Paints without robust UV inhibitor packages begin showing visible degradation within 18–24 months in direct sun exposure.

2. Triple-Digit Heat

When surface temperatures on south- and west-facing walls reach 150°F or higher in direct sun, low-quality paint films soften, blister, and eventually crack. High-solids acrylic paints form a more flexible, durable film that expands and contracts with these extremes without breaking down.

3. Dramatic Temperature Swings

The difference between a Redding afternoon and a Redding night in summer can be 45–55 degrees. Every cycle of expansion and contraction stresses the paint film. Paints with lower flexibility ratings develop micro-cracks along these stress lines, allowing moisture intrusion and accelerating failure — even in a dry climate like ours.

What to Look for in Exterior Paint for Hot Climates

When evaluating any exterior paint for use in Northern California, these are the non-negotiable specifications we screen for:

  • 100% acrylic latex binder: Acrylic outperforms vinyl-acrylic blends in UV and heat resistance. If the can says "latex" without specifying 100% acrylic, it likely contains vinyl-acrylic or styrene-acrylic, which degrade faster.
  • High solids content (40%+ by volume): Solids are the portion of the paint that remains on the wall after the carrier evaporates. Higher solids means a thicker, more protective dry film. Premium paints typically run 42–48% volume solids.
  • Built-in UV inhibitors: Look for paints that specifically advertise UV protection in their marketing. Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore both publish their UV-resistance data in technical data sheets — always worth checking.
  • Mildew resistance: Even in Redding's dry climate, morning dew and irrigation overspray can create enough moisture for mildew growth, particularly on north-facing walls and under eaves. Integrated mildewcides extend paint life significantly.
  • Self-priming or two-coat coverage: Premium paints with high hide ratios reduce the number of coats needed and build a thicker total film, improving both durability and heat resistance.

Top Paint Brands We Recommend

These are the three lines we specify most often on exterior repaint projects in Redding and Shasta County. All three are available at local Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore dealers.

Sherwin-Williams Emerald Exterior

This is our first-choice recommendation for most homes in Redding. Emerald Exterior is Sherwin-Williams' premium line and carries their strongest performance guarantees. It features ClimaGuard technology specifically engineered for temperature extremes, outstanding UV fade resistance, and a thick film build that covers most surfaces in two coats. The mildew-resistant formula also holds up well under Redding's oak tree and irrigation environments.

Sherwin-Williams Duration Exterior

Duration is Sherwin-Williams' mid-premium offering and a workhorse paint for budget-conscious homeowners who still want serious durability. It's a 100% acrylic with a lifetime limited warranty and performs very well in heat. It's not quite as UV-resistant as Emerald over a long horizon, but it's an excellent value and will outlast any big-box store brand by years in our climate.

Benjamin Moore Aura Exterior

Aura is Benjamin Moore's flagship exterior product and a genuine competitor to Emerald. Its Color Lock technology provides outstanding fade resistance, and the 100% acrylic formula handles Redding's thermal cycling very well. If you have a preferred Benjamin Moore dealer or a specific color that matches better in the Aura line, this is a paint we trust fully on high-exposure surfaces.

Sheen Matters Too

Choosing the right paint formula is only half the equation. The sheen level you select affects both durability and appearance, and the right choice depends on what surface you're painting.

Satin — Our Standard Recommendation for Siding

Satin sheen is the sweet spot for most exterior siding in Northern California. It provides enough sheen to repel moisture and dirt, is easy to clean, and doesn't show surface imperfections the way a semi-gloss would. It also holds color well under UV exposure. We default to satin for stucco, wood siding, lap siding, and fiber cement.

Semi-Gloss — Best for Trim, Doors, and Fascia

Semi-gloss is more durable and moisture-resistant, making it ideal for high-contact and high-exposure surfaces: window trim, door frames, entry doors, fascia boards, and shutters. The higher sheen also creates a pleasing visual contrast against a satin body color, which is a classic exterior design choice we see throughout Redding neighborhoods.

Flat — Only for Specific Applications

Flat exterior paint is occasionally used on masonry or rough stucco where hiding texture is the priority, but we rarely recommend it for primary siding in Redding. It shows dirt more readily, is harder to clean, and offers less moisture resistance — a liability even in a dry climate when morning dew and irrigation are factors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use interior paint on the outside of my house to save money?
No — and this is one of the most expensive mistakes a homeowner can make. Interior paints are not formulated to handle UV exposure, moisture, or thermal cycling. They will chalk, peel, and fail within one to two years in Redding's climate. Always use a paint explicitly labeled for exterior use.
Does paint color affect how long it lasts in the heat?
Yes, to a degree. Darker colors absorb more heat and can cause the paint film to soften and stress more rapidly on west- and south-facing walls. That said, with a premium 100% acrylic paint, the difference in longevity between light and dark colors is much smaller than it used to be. If you love a dark color, just make sure you're using a top-tier product like Emerald or Aura.
Should I prime before painting my exterior in Redding?
It depends on the condition of the existing surface. Bare wood, bare stucco, surfaces with stains, and areas where old paint has been removed always need a primer coat first. If the existing paint is in sound condition, a self-priming premium paint like Emerald or Duration can often skip a separate primer step — but we assess every surface before making that call. Proper priming is non-negotiable on new construction or after significant surface repairs.
