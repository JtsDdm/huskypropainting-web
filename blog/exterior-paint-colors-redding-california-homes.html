Best Exterior Paint Colors for Redding California Homes

By Husky Pro Painting · Redding, CA · Updated June 2025 · 6 min read

Choosing the right exterior color for your home in Redding is more than aesthetics — it's also about how that color performs under Northern California's intense sun. After painting hundreds of homes in Shasta County, we know which colors hold up, which ones fade fast, and what combinations look best against the oak-covered hills and warm light of the region.

Key principle: In Redding's climate, color and paint quality are equally important. A beautiful color in cheap paint will fade and chalk within 2–3 years. Always pair your color choice with a premium 100% acrylic paint rated for high UV exposure.

What Makes a Color Work in Northern California

Before picking a color, understand how Redding's environment affects exterior paint:

  • UV intensity: Shasta County receives some of the most intense sunlight in California. Saturated dark colors fade noticeably faster here than in coastal cities.
  • Heat reflection: Lighter colors reflect heat; darker colors absorb it. On west-facing walls that bake all afternoon, this matters for both paint longevity and home cooling costs.
  • Dust and red clay soil: Redding's dust settles on everything. Light neutrals and warm whites show less soil accumulation than stark whites.
  • Landscape context: The golden hills, granite outcrops, and oak trees of Northern California pair naturally with warm earthy tones more than cool grays or stark blues.

Our Top Color Picks for Redding Homes

Warm Neutrals — The Safe Choice That Always Works

Accessible Beige
Sherwin-Williams SW 7036

A warm greige (gray-beige) that reads differently in different light — more golden in morning sun, more neutral in shade. Works on stucco, wood, and brick. Pair with white trim and a dark brown or black front door for a classic Redding look.

Antique White
Sherwin-Williams SW 6119

A warm off-white with slight yellow undertones that complements the warm California light. Far more forgiving than stark bright white in Redding's intense midday sun, which can make pure white look washed out and highlight surface imperfections.

White Dove
Benjamin Moore OC-17

One of the most popular exterior whites in California. Slightly warm and creamy, it doesn't go stark white in bright sun. Looks elegant with dark charcoal or black trim — a combination we've used on dozens of homes in Redding and Shasta.

Earth Tones — Rooted in the Northern California Landscape

Dusty Miller
Sherwin-Williams SW 9166

A muted sage green that echoes the native vegetation of the foothills. Growing rapidly in popularity across Shasta County. Pairs beautifully with warm white trim and natural wood accents.

Rustic Red
Benjamin Moore HC-176

A muted, earthy red with brown undertones — not a bright fire-engine red, but a sophisticated terracotta that feels at home in the Northern California landscape. Best suited for homes with natural wood or stone elements.

Contemporary — Darker Tones Done Right

Iron Ore
Sherwin-Williams SW 7069

A dark charcoal with warm undertones. We see this increasingly used as an accent or trim color against white or light gray bodies — it creates a striking modern contrast. If used as a full body color, pair it with Emerald Exterior paint for maximum UV resistance.

Colors to Avoid in Redding's Climate

  • Stark bright white: Shows every imperfection and looks washed out in intense midday sun. A warm off-white is almost always better.
  • Deep navy or dark blue: Fades significantly within 3–5 years in high UV areas unless using top-tier paint. Also absorbs heat on west-facing walls.
  • Vibrant saturated colors (bright yellow, bright red): Fade quickly and can feel dated. Muted versions of the same hues are much more resilient.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exterior paint colors are most popular in Redding CA?
Warm neutrals — greige, soft white, and warm gray — are the most popular in Redding. Earth tones like sage green and muted terracotta are growing fast, complementing the natural landscape of Northern California.
Should I use light or dark colors in a hot climate like Redding?
Lighter colors are generally better because they reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat. Dark colors can fade faster and cause more thermal movement in wood, which stresses the paint film. If you want a dark color, use premium paint with strong UV inhibitors and expect to repaint sooner.
